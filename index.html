About

I'm a machine learning researcher at ASAPP Inc. My current research focuses on natural language processing problems in the customer service industry. I was born in Geneva, Switzerland and moved to the United States when I was sixteen. I attended the International School of Boston (ISB) for two years, where I now serve as a member of the Board of Trustees, assisting in the school governance, and alumni relations. I graduated from MIT in 2017, with a Master's in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, studying artifical intelligence, mathematics and neuroscience. While at MIT, I spent two years in Ed Boyden's Synthetic Neurobiology group, working on the application of Expansion Microscopy to Connectomics. I'm particulalry interested in the intersection between machine learning and neuroscience. I also love soccer, music, and going to a lot of restaurants.
